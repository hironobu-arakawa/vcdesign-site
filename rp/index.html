Resolution Protocol (RP)

RP (Resolution Protocol) is a pre-decision interlock. It prevents accidental promotion of AI outputs into the Responsible Domain.

RP is not a new philosophy.
RP is the specification that makes BOA’s “promotion requires explicit resolution” enforceable.

What RP is

RP is a promotion gate for Resolution. It is evaluated before anything is promoted into the Responsible Domain, especially when:

  • AI-generated candidates exist
  • evidence is incomplete or implicit
  • responsibility or ownership is unclear

If RP conditions are not satisfied, the system must fail safe.

What RP is not

RP is not:

  • an operational procedure / runbook
  • a manual for human actions
  • a mechanism that delegates or automates judgment
  • a way for AI to finalize decisions

RP defines constraints and failure modes, not steps.

Where RP applies in BOA

BOA separates artifacts into:

  • Fact (immutable observations)
  • Meaning (interpretation / hypothesis / suggestion)
  • Responsibility (actions and commitments)

RP applies at the boundary:

Meaning → Responsibility (promotion)

RP makes this boundary explicit and enforceable.

Fail-safe by design

RP is fail-safe by default:

  • Default outcome: deny promotion
  • If conditions are not met: quarantine or reject
  • AI may propose candidates, but cannot finalize resolution

This prevents silent actuation and accidental responsibility.

Specification

RP is defined as a normative protocol in the VCDesign Core:

  • VCDesign Core YAML: vcdesign_core.yaml

If you implement VCDesign, treat RP as a mandatory gate for any Resolution promotion.
