Definition

What is a Chapter?

A unit to delimit responsibility and context, not code.
Why "versions" and "phases" are not enough.

← Back to Manifesto Read VCD

1. Why "Versions" and "Phases" Are Not Enough

Software development already has excellent delimiters.
"Versions" to manage source code.
"Phases" to manage project progress.

These are perfect management units for the "maker".
However, when the system starts running and is exposed to the flow of reality, these units become powerless.
Because decisive information is missing there.

Missing Information:
"Who bears the weight of that judgment?"

Even if the version doesn't change, if the situation on the ground changes, the "meaning" of the system changes.
Even if the phase is complete, if the locus of responsibility is vague, operators are forced to continue making the most difficult decision of "doing nothing".

  • Versions delimit code, but not responsibility.
  • Phases delimit time, but not context.

That is why we needed a new unit.
A vessel to contain both the time of making and the time of continuing, and to pass the baton of responsibility without dropping it.
That is the Chapter.

2. Definition of Chapter

A chapter is not a functional grouping in a system.
It is the definition of "a declared period where the design guarantees value under a specific context and responsibility."

Three elements are packaged indivisibly in one chapter.

1. Context
Background information on "why this design was chosen" and "what was considered correct at the time".
It preserves the "agreement" on how the gap between the textbook "standard" and the "reality of the field" was interpreted and implemented at that time.
2. Boundary of Responsibility
A line drawn to define "up to where the system guarantees, and from where humans should judge".
The system cannot absorb all tremors of reality (delays, losses, exceptions).
A chapter explicitly states the treaty that "humans decide this tremor".
3. End & Next
This is the most important. A chapter always has an "End" defined.
Operation has no end, but a chapter has an end.
When "premises change" or "a certain period passes", the chapter closes, and responsibility is returned to the next chapter (or human).

3. Structure to Pass Through a Trembling World

The ideal (standard) of system development always assumes a "world that doesn't tremble".
That is not a defect, but a condition for establishment.
However, the field is always living in a "trembling world".
Time trembles, orders flip, and judgments change on the spot.

A Chapter is a "structure to pass tremors" placed between these two worlds.

Cutting the "Weight" of Operation

In operation fields, "judgment overload" often occurs, exhausting the field.
This is because all past judgments (compromises during design, temporary values during projects) continue to flow into operation without being organized.

By introducing chapters, this flow can be cut off.

  • "This judgment is based on the premises of Chapter 1."
  • "Since premises changed, Chapter 1 ends."

This releases operators from "infinite responsibility"
and allows them to focus on "judgments within the defined chapter".

4. New Job of Engineers: Editing Chapters

In an era where code is generated instantly by AI,
the engineer's job shifts from "writing content" to "defining chapters".

Engineers become Editors of Chapters
from Authors of code.

Questions as an Editor:
  • How long can this feature maintain value?
  • How far can this design take responsibility for the "tremors" of the field?
  • When it can no longer fulfill that responsibility, how do we safely return judgment to humans?

Deciding these and writing the "start" and "end" of the chapter into the blueprint.
That is the practice of Value Continuity Design proposed by VCDesign.

Epilogue — Closing the System Like a Book

Just as great books are composed of chapters,
great systems should also be composed of chapters.

Because there are chapters, we can stop for a moment.
Because there are chapters, we can notice that the context has changed.
And because there are chapters, we can turn a new page
while paying respect to the person who wrote the previous chapter (past self or team).

Even if the system is complete, value continues.
A milestone to avoid getting lost in that long journey.
That is the "Chapter".
