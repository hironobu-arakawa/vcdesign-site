Making, Fixing, and Continuing.

Build, fix, and continue.
The true reason why we have been so obsessed with "structure".

Prelude — Why Do Systems Turn Into "Debt"?

VCDesign (Value Continuity Design).
It is a design philosophy that deals not with "how to build", but how to protect the value after it is built.

In many projects, "completion" is the goal.
Meeting specifications, passing tests, celebrating the release.
That is considered success.

But years later, the reality left on the ground is harsh.
No one can explain why it is designed this way.
It works, but no one wants to touch the inside.
The original intent is forgotten, and the system becomes merely "a burden to be maintained."

The system survived.
But the value has not continued.

And the troublesome thing is,
No one witnessed the moment that value was lost.

It wasn't at completion, nor was it an outage.
It happens quietly, little by little,
During the time of daily operations.

VCDesign is an attempt to treat
That "disconnection of value that no one is watching"
As a structure.

The structure here does not mean hardening it rigid.
It is a mechanism so that even as time passes and people change,
The intent of "why we did this" and the responsibility continue to be handed over to the next.

1. What VCDesign Truly Envisioned

Even when the structure was complete, we kept asking ourselves.
Even if the system is complete, does the value truly continue?

Development has "phases", and software has "versions".
These are excellent delimiters for managing progress and recording changes.

However, as time went on, we began to feel a sense of unease.
Those delimiters do not tell us what a person experienced there.
Nor does it record how the meaning changed due to that experience.
And finally, it cannot explain when, where, and to whom responsibility was transferred.

Time is certainly flowing, but that flow does not remain in the design.
So, we questioned the unit of design itself.

What is a Chapter?
It is not merely a division of time or function.
A unit that includes human experience, changes in meaning, transitions of responsibility, and can contain both an "end" and a "next" simultaneously.

That was the concept of Chapter that we arrived at.

Passing Value Across Chapters

When the friction of "making" disappeared with AI, the center of gravity of engineering shifted quietly.
From "construction" to handing over value to the next stage without breaking it amidst the tremors caused by changes in reality.

A judgment made in one chapter becomes a premise in the next, eventually becoming a constraint, and someday a responsibility that must be returned.
To continue passing value across chapters without severing that chain.
That was the essence of the new engineering.

The Meaning of "Operation" Changes

Currently, the world simply calls this act "operation".
As maintenance after development is finished.

However, that will change.

In a world where "making" finishes instantly, continuing value after making (= operation) becomes the most creative and difficult job.

VCDesign is not a blueprint for making everything.
It is a map for judging "in which chapter responsibility should be returned to humans" in an era where the act called operation becomes the main character.

2. Definition of Future Engineer Functions

In a world where implementation costs approach zero with AI, what will engineers become?

Engineers are not "Builders"
Future engineers are neither builders nor finishers.
They will be The Continuer: someone who accepts the "tremors" caused by changes in reality and continues to tweak the finished product so that responsibility is not severed between people and systems.
Comparison Axis Old Image (The Builder) Future Image (The Continuer)
Fundamental Function "Builder" Fulfills functional requirements and delivers finished products. "Value Sustainer" Accepts reality's "tremors" and connects responsibility.
Attitude towards Completion Aim for "Completion" Treat changes (gaps) as bugs. Deny "Completion" "Continue tweaking" to return gaps without breaking.
Design Judgment Optimization and Extension Pursue "Is it technically possible?" Boundary of Responsibility Ask "Is it a responsibility intended to be taken?" and return it if unnecessary.
Value of Deliverable Feature List What can it do, how fast is it. Scope of Guarantee What is guaranteed, and where to return judgment to humans.

3. Invitation

With AI, theoretically "strongest designs" can always be created.
The reason we still don't choose them is because we would end up shouldering responsibilities we shouldn't.

Design is not optimization, but the judgment of accepting responsibility and returning responsibilities not accepted.
In a world where "being able to make" is no longer a value, we choose the path of "continuing to choose only designs where we can fulfill responsibility".
