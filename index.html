Why VCDesign exists

Most systems fail after they are delivered. Not because technology was wrong, but because meaning drifted, decisions were frozen too early, responsibility became implicit, and boundaries were never declared.

VCDesign exists to prevent that failure mode.

What VCDesign designs

VCDesign designs structure, not solutions.

Specifically: where decisions are allowed to live, where meaning must stop, where responsibility cannot escape, and where change is expected—not resisted.

If these are wrong, no architecture survives.

What VCDesign is not

VCDesign is not a framework, a methodology, a checklist, a reference architecture, or a product selection guide.

If you are looking for answers, this site will disappoint you. If you are deciding what must not break, it will help.

The Five Core Rules

VCDesign is built on five irreversible rules. These rules do not evolve. Everything else does.

  1. 1. Meaning is contextual The same words diverge across different worlds.
  2. 2. Outcomes require judgment Data alone never produces results.
  3. 3. Boundaries limit meaning Meaning can be defined beyond a boundary, but not fully carried.
  4. 4. Do not fix the unfixed What cannot be designed should not be frozen.
  5. 5. Storage follows flow Persistence is a consequence, not a starting point.

Relationship to construction methods

VCDesign sits above construction. Methods such as Boundary-Oriented Architecture (BOA) exist under VCDesign.

VCDesign defines why boundaries matter. Methods define how to build with them. VCDesign never mandates a method.

AI positioning

VCDesign is AI-aware, not AI-driven.

AI may assist by generating hypotheses, comparing interpretations, and exposing trade-offs. AI must never make final decisions, own responsibility, overwrite facts, or silently promote meaning.

Final judgment remains human-owned.

Who this is for

VCDesign applies to industrial systems, information systems, socio-technical systems, and AI-assisted decision environments.

It does not apply to systems that require zero ambiguity and zero human judgment.

How to read this site

Read it as constraints, not guidance.

VCDesign does not tell you what to build. It tells you where building the wrong thing becomes irreversible.

If you want the machine-readable core, look for vcd_core.yaml.

Frequently misunderstood

VCDesign is often misunderstood. If any of the following disappoints you, that is intentional.

  • Is VCDesign a framework or methodology? No. VCDesign defines constraints, not steps. It does not tell you what to do or how to do it.
  • Does VCDesign provide reference architectures? No. Reference architectures freeze decisions too early. VCDesign exists precisely to avoid that failure mode.
  • Can VCDesign be applied without BOA? Yes. BOA is one construction method under VCDesign. VCDesign never mandates a method.
  • Is VCDesign compatible with AI-driven systems? VCDesign is AI-aware, not AI-delegated. AI may generate hypotheses, but responsibility never transfers.
  • Why doesn’t VCDesign give concrete answers? Because answers belong to contexts. VCDesign protects the structure in which answers can be made safely.
